About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of robotics, biomedical engineering, and physical system modelling, with a focus on bio-inspired systems, system identification, and probabilistic state estimation. My work centres on sensorless force estimation and the modelling of dynamical interactions in medical robotics, with growing interest in exploiting the dynamics of physical systems for sensing, estimation, and control.

Currently, I work as a Foreign Expert at the Center for Biomedical and Robotics Technology (BART LAB), Faculty of Engineering, Mahidol University, focusing on bio-inspired needle systems for medical applications.

I am actively seeking research collaborations in the fields of medical robotics, sensor fusion, and control systems. I am passionate about mentoring students and sharing knowledge to help them grow in these fields.

Current Projects

  • Bio-inspired Needle System for Breast Biopsy with Force Estimation 2021-Present

    Developing an SMA-based embedded force sensing mechanism and adaptive state estimation framework for a bio-inspired dual-sheath needle system, originally developed for breast biopsy navigation. Supervised by Dr. Songpol Ongwattanakul and Dr. Jackrit Suthakorn (BART LAB), with Prof. Cholatip Wiratkapun (Department of Radiology, Ramathibodi Hospital) as clinical co-advisor.

  • Multi-Robot Collaboration for Disaster Response – Task-Oriented Link Length Optimization for Mobile Manipulators 2026-Present

    Kinematic design and workspace optimization of mobile manipulators for multi-task disaster response, applied to RoboCup Rescue-style manipulation tasks with an emphasis on real-world deployability.

  • Pole-Placement Control for DC Motor Actuation Ongoing

    Investigating error-dependent bandwidth shaping and analytical pole placement for precise closed-loop control of brushed DC servo motors, supporting actuation design for robotic and medical devices. (Manuscript submitted to Mechatronics.)
